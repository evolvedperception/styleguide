Voice and Writing Style

Voice Grammar & Mechanics Web Elements Word Usage

Voice

  • Write for everyone, remember some may just skim the content.
  • Create heirarchy of information on the page.
  • Lead with the most important content.
  • Avoid using slang.
  • Use positive rather than negative language.

Grammar and Mechanics

Acronyms

  • If an acronym is necessary for future reference, spell the full word followed by the acronym in parentheses on the first reference.
    • Example: Full Name Here (FNH)
  • If the abbreviation is well known and always recognizable use that instead of spelling it out.
    • Example: HTML

Capitalization

  • Capitalize all proper nouns, such as names of individuals and places.
  • Use all lowercase for email addresses and website URLs.
  • Do not capitalize words such as website, internet, online, email when they appear in the middle of a sentence.

Numbers

  • Spell out a number when it is at the beginning of a sentence.
  • Spell out a number if it is less than ten, otherwise use the numeral.
  • Numbers over three digits should contain a comma.

Dates

  • Spell out the day of the week and the month.
  • Only abbreviate the date if space is an issue.

Decimals and Fractions

  • Spell out fractions.
  • Use decimal points when a number can not easily be written out as a fraction.

Percentages

  • Use the % symbol instead of spelling out the word 'percent'.

Phone Numbers

  • Use dashes without spaces between numbers.
    • Example: 1-920-555-5555

Time

  • Use numerals and a or p, without a space in between when space is limited.
    • Example: 10:30a
  • Use numerals and a.m. or p.m., with a space in between.
    • Example: 10:30 a.m.
  • Use minutes for on-the-hour time.
    • Example: 10:00a
  • Use hypens between times to indicate a time period when using a or p.
    • Example: 10:00a-6:00p
  • Use 'to' between times to indicate a time period when using a.m. or p.m.
    • Example: 10:00 a.m to 6:00 p.m.
  • Specify time zones when writing about an event or something users would need to schedule.
  • Abbreviate time zones as follows:
    • Eastern time: ET
    • Central time: CT
    • Mountain time: MT
    • Pacific time: PT

Commas

  • When writing a list, use the Oxford comma.
    • Example: one, two, three, and four
    • Incorrect: one, two, three and four

Web Elements

Alt Text

  • Should be brief but descriptive of the image.

Buttons

  • Should always contain actions.
  • Use all caps for button text.

Menus

  • Use title case for menu options.

Forms

  • Form titles should be short and it should be clear what the purpose is.
  • Use title case for form titles and fields.

Links

  • Use links to point users to relevant content.
  • Links should appear different than the regular text.
  • Links should have hover and focus states to communicate they are interactive.

Word Usage

  • Log In should be two seperate words and title case.
    • Yes: Log In
    • No: login, signin, Sign In
  • website/webpage (no hyphen and lower case)
  • email (lower case and no hyphen)
